Want to be a counselor? Here's what you need to know

The Break for Jesus Organizing Committee is looking for some hard-working, fun-loving and creative teen counselors to assist in running this year’s programme.

You should:

  • Be in grades 10 to 12 or older
  • Belong to a Ukrainian Catholic Parish
  • Have several leadership qualities (hard working, mature, spiritual attitude, ability to work with children)
  • Camp and/or catechism experience is an asset.
  • Be available for March Break and counselor training (March 08 - 16, 2013)

Interested applicants are asked to fill in an application form, found here, and email it to b4j@gmx.com or mail to address given.



The following information MUST be included:

  • Why do I want to be a counselor?
  • Leadership experience (summer camps, retreats, catechism, youth group, previous B4J experience)
  • What can you offer to make the children’s experience memorable?
  • Level of Ukrainian: (none, minimal, medium or strong)
  • Reference: (priest, nun, religion teacher, or youth worker who knows you)


Mailing address:

Olia Dzurevych

6 Upminster Cres.
Toronto, ON M9B 5W2

Applications must be submitted by December 31, 2012 You will be notified by Jan. 31, 2013 if you have been selected as a counselor. All personal information provided will be kept confidential



Application for police check:

After you have been selected to be a counselor you must apply for a police check. You will be reimbursed for the cost of obtaining the check. Apply in person at your local police station, to obtain this check. Notify the police that this is a volunteer position. Allow 8-10 weeks for processing.

If you have previously been a counselor at B4J and have submitted a police check, please complete and send this form along with your application.
